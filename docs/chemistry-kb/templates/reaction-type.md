## Key Learnings

### What Works Well
- Pd(PPh3)4 at 3 mol% in THF/H2O (3:1) with K2CO3 base is our standard protocol
- Degassing solvents consistently improves yields by 5-15%
- Boronic acid pinacol esters are more stable than free boronic acids for long-running reactions
- Microwave conditions (100 °C, 30 min) can replace conventional heating (80 °C, 4 h) with comparable yields

### Common Pitfalls
- **Protodeboronation**: Electron-poor boronic acids decompose at high temperatures. Keep temperature at or below 80 °C for these substrates
- **Homocoupling**: Excess boronic acid (>1.5 equiv) promotes homocoupling. Use 1.2 equiv as standard
- **Catalyst Decomposition**: Old Pd(PPh3)4 gives variable results. Always check catalyst color before use
- **Oxygen Sensitivity**: Even trace oxygen causes catalyst deactivation. Use Schlenk technique, not just nitrogen balloon

### Substrate-Specific Advice
- **[[Heteroaryl Halides]]**: Pyridyl halides require slightly higher catalyst loading (3-5 mol%) due to nitrogen coordination
- **[[Electron-Deficient Arenes]]**: Fast oxidative addition but prone to protodeboronation; use mild conditions
- **[[Alkyl Boronic Acids]]**: Slow transmetalation; consider SPhos or XPhos ligands instead of PPh3
